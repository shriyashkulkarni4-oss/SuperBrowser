# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers of SuperBrowser pledge to make participation in our project and community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, colour, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behaviour that contributes to a positive environment:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experience
- Gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Crediting others' work and ideas appropriately

Examples of unacceptable behaviour:

- Harassment, trolling, or personal attacks in any project space
- Submitting AI-generated code without review (`gssoc:spam`)
- Claiming issues without following the contribution process outlined in `CONTRIBUTING.md`
- Dismissive or condescending comments on issues or pull requests
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission

---

## Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behaviour and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behaviour that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces — including the GitHub repository, issues, pull requests, and Discord — and also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces. Examples of representing the project include using the official project email, posting on social media about SuperBrowser, or speaking at an online or offline event on behalf of the project.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will follow these guidelines when determining consequences for violations:

### 1. Correction
**Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or other behaviour deemed unprofessional.

**Consequence:** A private written warning from the maintainer, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behaviour was inappropriate.

### 2. Warning
**Impact:** A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence:** A formal warning with consequences outlined for continued behaviour. No interaction with the people involved for a specified period of time.

### 3. Temporary Ban
**Impact:** A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behaviour.

**Consequence:** A temporary removal from all project spaces, including GitHub and Discord. No public or private interaction with the people involved is allowed during this period.

### 4. Permanent Ban
**Impact:** Demonstrating a pattern of violation, harassment, or aggression toward individuals or groups.

**Consequence:** A permanent ban from all project spaces and public community interaction.
